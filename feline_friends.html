Feline Friends: A Specialist Retail Product

Feline Friends is an online retail product, targeting cat owners. This would be a ‘one stop shop’, allowing cat owners to find varied brands and products as well as nearby specialist services, all using their device. Wherever and whenever they need to!

My Process

The User Centred Design process was followed; from discovery, to concepting to prototyping and user testing. UX and UI design disciplines were utilised using Balsamiq and Sketch as design tools.

Research & Ideation

I was provided with an initial project brief and baseline user stories. From this I researched some well known competitors such as ‘Pets at Home’ and developed a branding guidelines document and user flows based on the stories in scope.

Crazy 8s were then generated to gather a larger number of ideas in a short space of time.

User research also uncovered key areas user’s wanted to see from an initial release of the app; such as logging, easy search with media clips and clear imagery as well as the ability to save recipes that could easily be found again later on.

Wireframe

Initial low fidelity wireframes were sketched out, using selected designs from the crazy 8s exercise. These were based on the user flows created.

Design

The sketches were migrated into digital design using Balsamiq. The designs were developed further into mid-fidelity, incorporating more detailed elements and interactions.

Prototype

A prototype was created using the Balsamiq designs and tested on three different users that would represent the target market. Valuable feedback was gathered and the outcome was a backlog of changes that could either be implemented in the initial release or ‘parked’ for future development.

Final Designs

Final designs were generated in Sketch, incorporating high priority items captured during testing. This included the design of a desktop version as the product was a responsive design.

Conclusion

Is the design TOO simplistic?

No! The test participants liked the simplistic design and it made it easier to navigate and understand.

Lesson Learnt: If in doubt, always test!

Will using Balsamiq for mid-fidelity mean it takes longer to get to the final design in Sketch?

Actually, using Balsamiq meant I could quickly create a good quality prototype that could easily be shared with participants via email. The time saved here meant I could afford the time spent migrating the design into sketch!

Lesson Learnt: Balsamiq is great for quick low to mid-fidelity designs and good for prompt prototyping as well!
